Biography

Steve Jobs was a visionary entrepreneur and inventor who co-founded Apple Inc. He revolutionized the tech industry with products like the Macintosh, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. Born in San Francisco in 1955, Jobs’ passion for technology and design led him to change the world through innovation. He also played a crucial role in Pixar Animation Studios before his passing in 2011, leaving a lasting legacy of creativity and innovation.

Timeline

  • 1955

    Steve Jobs was born in San Francisco, California.

  • 1976

    Co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne.

  • 1985

    Left Apple and founded NeXT Inc.

  • 1997

    Returned to Apple as interim CEO, leading the company back to profitability.

  • 2001

    Introduced the first iPod, revolutionizing portable music.

  • 2007

    Launched the first iPhone, transforming the smartphone industry.

  • 2011

    Passed away, leaving a profound legacy in technology and design.

Legacy & Impact

  • Co-founded Apple Inc. and revolutionized personal computing.
  • Introduced the Macintosh computer with a graphical user interface.
  • Launched revolutionary products like the iPod, iPhone, and iPad.
  • Transformed the music industry with iTunes and the iTunes Store.
  • Helped Pixar Animation Studios become a leading animation company.
  • Inducted into the California Hall of Fame and awarded numerous honors.
